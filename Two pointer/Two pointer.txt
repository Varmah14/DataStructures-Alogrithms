What is the Two Pointers Technique?

The two pointers technique is a problem-solving approach where you use two indices (pointers) 
to traverse a data structure (usually an array or string) from different directions or at different speeds.

Why use it?

It reduces time complexity (often from O(n²) → O(n)).

Helps avoid nested loops by scanning once with two pointers.

Typical patterns

Opposite ends → One pointer at the start, one at the end. Move inward based on conditions.
Example: check if a string is a palindrome, two-sum in sorted array.

Same direction → Both pointers move forward, but at different speeds or under different conditions.
Example: fast and slow pointers (Floyd’s cycle detection), sliding window problems.

Fixed vs. moving pointer → One pointer holds the "anchor," while the other explores forward.
Example: remove duplicates from sorted array.
